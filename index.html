Modelling the physics of musical instruments

A collection of finite-element and modal-analysis projects exploring how instruments produce, radiate, and shape sound — built mostly in COMSOL Multiphysics and MATLAB.

The Musical Triangle — Forming Residuals & Acoustic Response

A study of the steel triangle as a physical and acoustic object: simulating the bending/forming process to visualise residual stresses, alongside the impact mechanics and modal behaviour that govern its characteristic sound.

Reduced-order acoustic modelling via Component Mode Synthesis, including a mode-importance criterion based on coupling to Modal Constraint Modes (MCMs) for deciding which modes a reduced model must retain. Presented as a conference paper.

Piezo Impact Sensing for Instrument Excitation

A low-cost piezo-disc impact sensor (Arduino Uno) for capturing the strike that excites an instrument, paired with a browser-based Web Serial visualiser to inspect the impulse and its frequency content in real time.
